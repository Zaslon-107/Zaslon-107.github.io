Interview

Introduction

Today, we will be interviewing Preetam kaur, wife of Ranjit Singh who was the interviewee of the first blog post of this blog. She will be sharing her knowledge on her specialty occupation as a blockchain specialist and project manager at Mastercard UK.

Questions:

Q1. What do you see as the future prospects for software developers and project managers in the tech industry? Q2. What was the path that you took to get into this profession?
Q3. Can you provide an idea of the salary range for software developers and project managers at Mastercard? Q4. How do you feel at the end of the day?
Q5. What do you think of in worries that how AI might take over this job?
Q6. What would help me prepare for this career?

Question 1:

What was your journey on getting into this career?

My journey was a similar one to that of my husband, in that we both had studied and got degrees and certifications from universities in Punjab, and we started work pretty early on in the software sector. I had to move to the UK immediately following my husband’s relocation to Mastercard, and this was right after I had finished my degree in the MCA program. This degree was all about computer science and the inner workings of computer systems and software. This degree would be roughly equivalent to a computer science degree here in the west. After the relocation, I was employed by a private networking company from where I gained some experience and was able to get into mastercard and specialize in the new blockchain technology at that time.

Question 2:

What skills and qualifications are most important for a career in such careers like blockchain development and related fields?

To start off, I would say that proficiency in languages like Solidity, JavaScript, and Python is crucial to developing smart contracts and blockchain applications. Knowledge wise, a deep understanding of cryptographic principles and distributed ledger technology(synchronised and distributed digital data) is necessary to ensure security and efficiency to all users of your platform. Practical experience through projects or degrees and continuous learning to stay updated with the latest technology will significantly help your career prospects in such a rapidly evolving field as blockchain is.

Question 3:

What does a typical day look like in your role?

A typical day in my role involves a majority of technical and collaborative tasks. I start by reviewing project updates and addressing any urgent issues and upsets with my team. Throughout the day we collaborate with developers to design and create blockchain solutions, for our financial division and to meet our security and performance standards.

Question 4:

How do you see this industry evolving over the next few years?

The blockchain industry is predicted to have lots of growth and transformation over the next few years. You can expect to see increased adoption of blockchain technology in various sectors, mainly finance, supply chain, and healthcare. Innovations in scalability, compatibility, and regulatory frameworks will drive mainstream acceptance and integration. also, the rise of decentralized finance (DeFi) and non-fungible tokens (NFTs) will continue to create new opportunities for the newer generations.

Question 5:

What advice would you give to someone just starting out in this career?

For someone thats just starting out in a blockchain career, my advice would be to focus on getting good in computer science and cryptography. Get hands-on experience with blockchain platforms like Ethereum and Hyperledger, and learn programming languages such as Solidity and Python. Staying updated with the latest trends and advancements in the blockchain space is also very important, as it's a rapidly evolving field. And lastly, you shouldn’t underestimate the value of networking which means to connect with professionals in the industry like me, attend conferences, and participate in online forums to learn more about such fields.
