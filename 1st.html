Question 3:

Can you provide an idea of the salary range for software developers and project managers at Mastercard?

My salary range for working here at Mastercard UK is around £600 000 pounds which is very high in comparison to the junior devs around here , but this is because I have worked here for around 20 years now since the early 2000s. You can expect the salary for junior devs and other entry level positions to be around £40k-£70k here in the UK while more experienced devs will have much more pay as a result of thier extensive career and expertise.

Question 5:

What do you think of in worries that how AI might take over this job?

I personally believe the opposite because for us devs, we see AI as just tools and productivity enhancers. Sure, AI might give you all the code you might need and will even reslove bugs and other issues, but it simply can't create whole programs and systems by itself, esspecially massive ones like what we make here at our big firms. We let the AIs write out the low-level code and have our guys structure and debug it, again, it only serve as tools and boost our productivity to get more done in less time.

Question 6:

What would help me prepare for this career?

To prepare for a career in software programming you have to first build a strong foundation in programming, data structures, and debugging. Start with languages like Python or Java and then learn to program in different styles such as Object-oriented programming , Functional programming,etc. Keep yourself updated with the latest technologies and trends in the industry. Practical experience from internships or large-scale projects is the most important as is it gives job-like experience. Most importantly, consistency and practice is the single most stressed and important thing in getting better at programming.
